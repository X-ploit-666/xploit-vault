The Internet Archive

In a digital world where websites vanish in an instant, the Wayback Machine serves as the internet's memory. Operated by the non-profit Internet Archive, it allows us to travel back in time to view "snapshots" of the World Wide Web as it once existed.

> MISSION: Preserve the digital history of the web for researchers, historians, and security analysts.

Anatomy of a Snapshot

The Wayback Machine doesn't just index text; it downloads the entire rendering package.
