The Digital Spider

Crawling is the automated process of browsing the World Wide Web. Like a spider navigating its web, a Crawler (or bot) systematically follows links to discover, index, and analyze the internet's vast data.

Automated

Operates without human intervention using algorithms.

Systematic

Follows real paths, distinguishing it from random guessing.

Recursive

Finds links, visits them, finds more links, and repeats.

Strategic Approaches

Crawlers don't just wander aimlessly; they follow a strategy. The choice depends on whether the goal is a broad map of the site or finding specific assets buried deep within the structure.

Breadth-First (Wide)

Explores the entire surface level before moving down.

  • Prioritizes website structure mapping.
  • Visits all neighbors of the seed first.

Depth-First (Deep)

Drills down a single path until it hits a dead end.

  • Good for finding specific hidden content.
  • Backtracks only when necessary.
