Constructing an Attack Library

An Attack Library is a structured collection of hacker methods. When building or choosing one, you face a critical trade-off between just Collecting Tools (weaponized code) and Abstraction (finding the general pattern).

  • ►

    Collecting Tools

    Requires high effort to see the "big picture" every time. It's just raw exploit code.

  • ►

    Abstraction

    Turns tools into general lessons ("how" and "why"). Easier to use for sparking new ideas.

The CAPEC Data Structure

The Common Attack Pattern Enumeration and Classification (CAPEC) isn't code. It's a highly detailed template used to describe an attack comprehensively. Every CAPEC entry breaks down a threat into these six actionable parts.

01

Summary

A short, high-level explanation defining what the attack actually is.

02

Execution Flow

The step-by-step "story" of how the hacker actively performs the attack.

03

Prerequisites

Conditions the hacker needs before starting (e.g., "User must be logged in").

04

Probing Techniques

How the attacker safely "pokes" the system to discover if a weakness exists.

05

Indicators / Warnings

The "red flags" and system logs that prove an attack is currently underway.

06

Mitigations

The architectural or code-level "fix" required to stop the attack from succeeding.

Privacy vs. Security

A security threat breaks the rules of a system. A privacy threat occurs when someone's personal information is used in a way they didn't authorize or desire. Understanding the distinct vectors of privacy harms is crucial.

Contextual Integrity

Privacy is violated when data moves across contexts (e.g., medical secrets shared in a workplace context).

Linkability

The danger of joining isolated databases (e.g., linking movie habits to a home address).

Distribution of Privacy Harms

The Nymity Slider

Nymity measures how much of your "identity" is exposed during a transaction. Designing systems that default to the left side of the slider preserves user privacy significantly more than systems on the right.
