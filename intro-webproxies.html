WEB PROXIES

The essential "Man-in-the-Middle" tools for modern penetration testing. Intercept, analyze, and secure the conversation between user and server.

How It Works

Modern applications don't store everything on your device. They constantly talk to back-end servers to process data. To secure these servers, we must test the messages sent to them.

The "Man-in-the-Middle"

A Web Proxy sits between your browser and the server. It captures HTTP (Port 80) and HTTPS (Port 443) traffic, allowing you to pause, view, and modify requests before they reach their destination.

Core Utilities

Beyond just "catching" messages, proxies are versatile toolkits. They automate the finding of security holes ("Scanning"), send massive amounts of random data to break things ("Fuzzing"), and map out the entire structure of a website.

  • Interception: Manual editing of data.
  • Fuzzing: Stress testing inputs.
  • Crawling: Finding all pages.
