In-Depth Analysis: The 5 OAT Subsets

Below is a detailed breakdown of each automated threat subset. By mapping these threats, security analysts can better tune rate limiting, CAPTCHA challenges, and behavioral analytics.

👤

Account & Identity

Abuse of login, registration, and profile management systems.

  • OAT-007 Credential Cracking
  • OAT-008 Credential Stuffing
  • OAT-019 Account Creation
  • OAT-020 Account Aggregation
💳

Payment & Carding

Attacking the checkout, credit card forms, and financial data.

  • OAT-001 Carding
  • OAT-010 Card Cracking
  • OAT-012 Cashing Out
🔍

Discovery & Vulnerability

Probing the application to see how it's built and where it's weak.

  • OAT-004 Fingerprinting
  • OAT-018 Footprinting
  • OAT-014 Vulnerability Scanning
📦

Inventory & Content Abuse

Stealing data or manipulating the stuff the website sells or displays.

  • OAT-005 Scalping
  • OAT-011 Scraping
  • OAT-013 Sniping
  • OAT-017 Spamming
📈

Metric & Process Manipulation

Messing with the site's numbers or cheating the system logic.

  • OAT-002 Token Cracking
  • OAT-003 Ad Fraud
  • OAT-006 Expediting
  • OAT-015 Denial of Service
  • OAT-016 Skewing

Future Outlook & Conclusion

As defensive perimeters harden against traditional injection attacks, adversaries increasingly rely on automation to abuse intended functionality. Understanding the 19 OWASP Automated Threats ensures that your bug bounty hunting and security architecture are aligned with modern attack patterns. Stop looking just for bugs, and start looking for business logic flaws.
