The "Protecting Your Home" Analogy

Threat modeling sounds technical, but it’s an instinct you already use. Imagine securing your house:

💎

1. The Good Stuff

Identifying what is valuable (family, photos, electronics).

🔓

2. The Problems

Finding the weak spots (unlocked doors, open windows).

🦹‍♂️

3. The "Bad Guys"

Knowing who might break in (thieves, kids, stalkers).

Software works exactly the same way. We must identify what's valuable and protect it from digital intruders.

Why Do We Do It?

Finding and fixing security problems isn't just about safety; it's about efficiency and trust.

  • ✓
    Find Mistakes Early It is significantly cheaper to fix a flaw during design than after launch.
  • ✓
    Understand Needs Determine if you need bank-level security or just a sturdy lock.
  • ✓
    Build Better Products Proactive safety measures increase reliability and customer trust.

The 4-Step Framework

Any security project can be handled effectively by following this simple, structured loop.

🗺️

1. What are we building?

Draw a map or diagram of how your app works.

🔍

2. What can go wrong?

Look at the map and find the weak spots.

🛠️

3. What should we do?

Decide how to mitigate or fix those weak spots.

✅

4. Did we do a good job?

Double-check everything to avoid missing anything.

Key Terms & Takeaways

The Dictionary

Abstraction
Looking at a simplified version of a complex system to see the "big picture".
Mitigation
A fancy word for a "fix" or a way to reduce a risk.
Attack Tree
A diagram showing the different paths a bad guy could take to reach a goal.
Bug vs. Flaw
A Bug is an error in the code. A Flaw is a mistake in the overall design or plan.

Final Thoughts

🧠

Don't "think like an attacker"

Most people find that hard. Instead, use structured lists (like STRIDE) and questions to guide you.

🎻

It's a Skill

Like playing the violin, you get better at threat modeling the more you practice it.

⚖️

Pragmatism over Perfection

You don't need a mathematically perfect system; you just need one that is useful and makes your software safer.
